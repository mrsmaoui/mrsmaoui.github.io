Aim

Get ready to ignite your passion for Computer Science! Join us for upcoming events tailored for undergraduate and graduate students in the Computer Science Innovation Lab.

  • Workshops: Workshops on various topics such as web development, software development, and operating systems can help students learn new skills and improve their knowledge.
  • Hackathons: Hosting hackathons to encourage students to develop and implement their own projects.
  • Student Showcase: A platform for students to present their projects and get feedback from their peers and industry experts.
  • Guest Speaker Series: Inviting industry experts to share their knowledge and experiences with the students.
  • Career Fairs: Hosting career fairs to connect students with potential employers and help them learn about the job market and available opportunities.
  • Internship & Job Opportunities: Providing students with opportunities to work on projects, complete internships, and gain hands-on experience in their field.

In addition to the events above, graduate students at the Computer Science Innovation Lab are welcome to assist the lab staff in conducting the following events:

  • Research Symposium: These research oriented gatherings provide a platform for graduate students to showcase their research projects and receive feedback from peers, faculty members, and industry partners.
  • Workshops: Hosting workshops on specific topics relevant to the areas of focus of the lab helps students gain practical skills and knowledge.
  • Technical Talks: Inviting experts from academia and industry to give presentations on cutting-edge technologies and developments in the field of computer science provides valuable insights and networking opportunities for graduate students.
  • Networking Opportunities: Organizing networking events where graduate students can interact with industry partners and alumni helps them form valuable connections for their future careers.
  • Career Fair: Hosting a career fair specifically for graduate students to meet with potential employers and explore job opportunities in the field of computer science.
